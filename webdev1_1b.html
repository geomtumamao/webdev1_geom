Filipino-style Chicken Adobo



Chicken Adobo is a popular Filipino dish known for its savory and slightly tangy flavors. Chicken Adobo is traditionally served with steamed rice. You can spoon the chicken and sauce over the rice, and enjoy the flavorful, tender chicken pieces.

Here's a recipe for Filipino-style Chicken Adobo:
Serves 4-6 people.


Ingredients:



  • One (1) kg chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks work well)
  • Half (1/2) cup soy sauce
  • Half (1/2) cup white vinegar
  • Four (4) to six (6) cloves garlic, minced
  • One (1) teaspoon black peppercorns
  • Two (2) to three (3) bay leaves
  • Two (2) tablespoons cooking oil (vegetable or canola)
  • One (1) onion, chopped
  • One (1) cup water
  • One (1) to two (2) potatoes, peeled and sliced (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps:



  1. In a large bowl or container, combine the chicken pieces, soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, black peppercorns, and bay leaves.
  2. Mix everything well, ensuring the chicken is coated with the marinade.
  3. Cover and refrigerate for at least thirty (30) minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the chicken. For better results, marinate for a few hours or overnight.
  4. Heat the cooking oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pan or Dutch oven over medium-high heat.
  5. Remove the chicken pieces from the marinade, but keep the marinade for later use.
  6. Sear the chicken pieces in the hot oil until they turn golden brown on all sides. This step adds flavor and texture to the dish.
  7. Once the chicken is browned, add the chopped onion and saut√© until it becomes translucent.
  8. Pour in the marinade (soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, peppercorns, and bay leaves) into the pan with the chicken and onions.
  9. Add one (1) cup of water to the pan and bring everything to a boil.
  10. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken becomes tender. If using potatoes, add them to the simmering mixture about halfway through the cooking time.
  11. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed. Keep in mind that the soy sauce is salty, so be cautious with the salt.
  12. Optional: If you want a variation called "Adobong Manok Sa Gata," you can add coconut milk (about 1 cup) during the last 10 minutes of simmering. This will give the dish a creamy and slightly sweet flavor.
  13. Garnish your Chicken Adobo with some chopped fresh cilantro, green onions, or fried garlic for extra flavor and presentation.
